Features

A studio and an editor, in one window

Capture and edit in the same native app. No round-trips, no uploads, no subscription.

Screen + camera recorder

Capture a display, window, or region — plus your camera and mic. System audio and voice are recorded together, cleanly.

Camera as a layer

Square PiP with corner presets, or a true split / side-by-side layout. Center Stage keeps you framed. Switch positions with one click.

Edit by transcript

On-device transcription turns your words into an editable script. Delete a sentence to cut it from the video — like Descript, but local.

Cinematic cursor zoom

Automatic, smooth zoom-ins that follow your cursor and clicks — the Screen Studio look — fully editable as ranges on the timeline.

Captions & backgrounds

Styled, burned-in captions and macOS-style gradient backgrounds with padding. Pick a preset, ship it.

One-click reel export

Export 9:16, 1:1, or 16:9 with captions burned in and an SRT sidecar. Frame-accurate timeline, instant cut at the playhead.

How it works

From record to reel in three steps

Record

Pick a screen, window, or your camera. Choose a reel layout — PiP, split, or side-by-side — and hit record. Stop from the floating bar.

Edit

Slipstream transcribes on-device. Trim by deleting words, add zooms and captions, position the camera with presets, drop a background.

Export

Choose your aspect ratio and export a finished short with captions baked in. Everything renders locally — nothing is uploaded.

FAQ

Good to know

Is it really free?

Yes. This first version is free and distributed openly. No account, no watermark, no usage caps.

Does my video get uploaded anywhere?

No. Recording, transcription, editing and export all happen on your Mac. Slipstream has no servers and makes no network calls with your footage.

The app says it "can't be opened" on first launch — why?

The first free build isn't notarized by Apple yet, so Gatekeeper is cautious. Right-click the app → Open → Open, or run xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/Slipstream.app once.

What do I need to run it?

Pick the build for your platform: Windows x64, macOS 26.4 or later, or Linux x64 via AppImage/DEB. Camera effects like Center Stage and Portrait need supported Mac camera hardware and are captured automatically by the macOS build.

How is this different from Descript, Screen Studio, OpusClip…?

Those each own one slice — a recorder, a captioner, or a cloud repurposer. Slipstream does capture, transcript editing, cursor zoom, camera layouts, captions and export in one native, offline, free app. See the comparison above.
